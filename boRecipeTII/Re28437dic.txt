Baked Noodles with Raisins and Nuts (Lokshen Kugel)
noodles, Butter or margarine, Raisins, Salt, Eggs, Cinnamon, Apples, Bread crumbs, Sugar, Walnuts, 
Rabbi Pinchas, the Tzaddik (wise man) of Koritz, used to say that Jews eat  lots of lokshen on Shabbat because noodles are symbolic of the unity of the  people of Israel: They are so entangled that they can never be separated.    1. Preheat oven to hot (400F)    2. Cook noodles according to package directions until tender. Drain. 3.  Beat eggs yolks with sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg and salt. Add drained noodles,  butter or margarine, raisins or chopped apples and walnuts and mix well. 4.  Beat egg whites until stiff and gently fold into noodle mixture. Pour into  greased casserole dish, dot with butter or margarine and cover with bread  crumbs. Bake 45 minutes.
