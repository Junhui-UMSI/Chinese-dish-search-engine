Watermelon Granita
Fresh lemons, Watermelon, Campari, Sugar, Salt, 
Subject: recipelu Good Morning American Recipes    1. Remove the rind from the watermelon and proceed to seed it and chop it  the watermelon into small chunks. (approximately 5 cups)    2. Place the watermelon chunks along with 1/2 cup of freshly squeezed lemon  juice, derived from the 3 fresh lemons, and 1/4 of sugar into a food  processor. Puree the mixture until smooth.    3. Remove the top of the food processor and add 3 tablespoons of Campari  and      1/8    teaspoon of salt.    4. Pour the watermelon mixture into a chilled rectangular metal baking pan.  Place the pan in the freezer for 30 minutes or until ice crystals form  around the sides and bottom of pan.    5. With a fork, gently rake the mixture until the frozen and unfrozen  elements are evenly combined. Break up any large clumps that form, but be  careful of making a slush.    6. Return the pan to the freezer and repeat the raking process every 20  minutes until the ice has formed a uniform consistency of soft crushed ice.  The entire process will take about two hours. (Some freezers may vary).    Serving the Granita    Serve the granita in a Martini glass, topping with fresh fruit.    To create a sparkling drink, fill a tall glass with granita and top with  seltzer or sparkling water    MC Formatted and MC Busted by Barb at PK on 3/14/98  Posted to recipelu-digest by "abprice@wf.net" <abprice@wf.net> on Mar 15,  1998
