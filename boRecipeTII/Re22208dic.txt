Turkey Sausage and Black-Eyed Pea Stew (Light)
Smoked turkey sausage,, Cayenne pepper, Long-grain white rice, (15-oz) black-eyed peas, Chopped fresh sage leaves, (15-oz) peeled and diced, Salt, Red or yellow bell pepper,, Rice, Water, Canola oil, (or vegetable, Paprika, CAJUN SEASONING, Dried basil, Flour, Garlic, Freshly ground black pepper,, Dried thyme, Garlic powder, Onion, Onion powder, STEW, Fresh parsley, finely, Celery ribs, finely chopped, Low-sodium chicken broth, 
1. To prepare the rice: Combine the water, rice and salt in a medium  saucepan. Bring to a boil and continue boiling, uncovered, until the level  of the liquid just covers the rice. Stir occasionally. Cover and reduce the  heat to low. Cook 15 minutes. Set aside, covered.    2. To prepare the Cajun seasoning: Combine the thyme, basil, paprika,  cayenne, onion powder, garlic powder and flour. set aside.    3. To prepare the stew: In a large saucepan or skillet, preferably  nonstick, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic; saute 3  minutes. Add the celery, bell pepper and 2 tablespoons of the broth. cover  and cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.    4. Add the turkey sausage and saute with the vegetables, uncovered, 5  minutes. Then sprinkle on the Cajun seasoning and cook 1 minute.    5. Add the remaining broth and the tomatoes. Cook 5 minutes, stirring  occasionally.    6. Drain and rinse the black-eyed peas; drain well again. Put into the pan  with the sage, cover and cook over medium-low heat 10 minutes, stirring  often.    7. Stir in the parsley and pepper. Adjust the seasonings if necessary and  serve over rice.    Adapted from "On Rice -- 60 Fast and Easy Toppings That Make the Meal" by  Rick Rogers.
