Pork with Pineapple Salsa
Dijon style mustard, Pork tenderloin; trimmed, Finely chopped red or green, Paprika, Crushed hot red pepper, Diced fresh pineapple or 1, Firmly packed light brown, Unsweetened pineapple juice, Ground ginger, Sliced green onion, 
Source: Healthy Meals in Minutes  1.. Preheat oven to 450F. In a small bowl, combine 2 Tbsp of brown sugar,  mustard, paprika, and ginger. Mix well.    2.. Tie the pork at intervals with kitchen twine. Place on the rack in  broiler pan; spread evenly with half of brown sugar mixture. Roast for 15  minutes.    3.. Spread pork with remaining brown sugar mixture; roast until an instant  read meat thermometer inserted in thickest part of meat registers 160F.,  about 20 minutes.    4.. In a medium bowl, combine the remaining brown sugar, pineapple,  pineapple juice, bell pepper, green onion and red pepper flakes.    5.. Transfer pork to a serving platter; let stand for 5 minutes. Remove and  discard kitchen twine. Slice and serve with the salsa.    Serves: 6    Calories: 171 (21% from fat) Carbohydrates: 13g Protein: 20g Sodium: 194mg  Fat: 4g Cholesterol: 56mg    Posted to EAT-LF Digest by "Lisa Whittington" <esordliw@pacbell.net> on Mar  7, 1998
