Phyllo Pear Pie
FILLING, Non-stick cooking spray, Each ground cinnamon,, Sheets phyllo dough, thawed, Apple juice, Ripe pears (about 2 3/4, Packed light-brown sugar, Granulated sugar, Lemon juice, All-purpose flour, Crust, Granulated sugar, 
1. Heat oven to 400 F. Have ready a 9-inch pie plate and a jelly-roll pan  (to catch drips).    2. Quarter, peel and core pears. Cut each quarter crosswise in  1/2-inch-thick slices. Put pears and remaining Filling ingredients in a  large, heavy saucepan.    3. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover and simmer 10 to 12 minutes,  stirring occasionally, until pears are tender but not mushy. Spoon mixture  into 9-inch pie plate. Let cool slightly.    4. Lay 1 sheet phyllo over filling. Spray all over with nonstick cooking  spray. Lay second sheet crosswise over first and spray. Continue layering  and spraying remaining 2 sheets phyllo, slightly overlapping each one.    5. Gather edges of phyllo and rest on rim of pie plate to make a ruffled  edge. Spray edges, then sprinkle entire top with remaining teaspoon sugar.  Cut slits through all layers phyllo for steam to escape while baking.    6. Place pie plate on jelly-roll pan. Bake 12 minutes or until phyllo is  golden brown. Cool on wire rack. Serve warm or at room temperature.
