Traditional Roll Dough
Shortening or margarine or, Sugar, Margarine or butter, soften, All-purpose* or unbleached, Very warm water, Egg, Salt, Regular or quick-acting, Very warm milk, 
*if using self-rising flour  Omit salt.    This basic dough lets you make a cornucopia of rolls, from Fan Tans. Mix 2  cups of the flour, the sugar, shortening, salt and yeast in medium bowl.  Add warm water, warm milk and egg. Beat on low speed 1 minute, scraping  bowl frequently. Beat on medium speed 1 minute, scraping bowl frequently.  Stir in enough remaining flour to make dough easy to handle. Turn dough  onto lightly floured surface. Knead about 5 minutes or until smooth and  elastic. Place in greased medium bowl and turned greased side up. Cover and  let rise in warm place about 1 hour or until double. (Dough is ready if  indention remains when touched.)Punch down dough. Cut or roll as directed  for any of the variations. Brush with margarine. Cover and let rise about  30 minutes or until double.Heat oven to 400 degrees. Bake 12 to 18 minutes  or until golden brown.You can make a whole wheat version of the dough by  substituting 1-1/2 to 1-3/4 cups whole wheat flour for the second addition  of all-purpose flour.
