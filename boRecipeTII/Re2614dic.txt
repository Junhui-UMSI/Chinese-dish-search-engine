Peanut Brittle
Butter, Sugar, Baking soda; heaping, Glucose; or karo, Vanilla, Peanuts, Water, 
Put sugar, glucose and water on hot fire and stire until it commences  to boil. Wash down sides of kettelt. Cover until it steams well.  Remove cover and cook until it cracks when dropped in cold water. Put  in peanuts, butter, stir constantly. Cook until peanuts are roasted  and candy becomes a golden brown. Sometimes peanuts commence to pop,  which indicates they are roasted sufficiently. Use raw peanuts. When  peanuts are roasted, set off fire and stir vanilla in well. Have your  soda dissolved in just a little water, using only enough to cover  immediately. After adding vanilla, pour in dissolved soda and stir  thoroughly. As soon as it is stirred thoroughly, pour out on warm  greased plate or slab. Cost of making about 34 cents. Makes 2 pounds.    Source: Margaret Foster, Keene Hill Grange, Coshocton County, OH  Originally published in Ohio State Grange Cook Book, 21st ed., Apr 1966.
