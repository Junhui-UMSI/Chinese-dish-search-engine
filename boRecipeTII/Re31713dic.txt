Swordfish Involtini Messinese
Salted anchovies, rinsed;, Basic tomato sauce, Virgin olive oil, Pine nuts; plus 1/4 cup, Oranges; zested, Swordfish, each about 1/4", Sicilian black olives, Currants; plus 1/4 cup, Bread crumbs, Parsley leaves, Spanish onion, chopped;, Hot chilies, White wine, 
Preheat oven to 450 F.    Flatten each piece of swordfish between sheets of oiled wax paper or  aluminum foil until 1/8-inch thick and lay out on a cool surface.    In a 12- to 14-inch saute pan, heat 4 tablespoons olive oil until smoking  and add Spanish onion, anchovies, 2 tablespoons pine nuts, and 2  tablespoons currants. Cook until onion has softened and lightly browned and  add orange zest and bread crumbs. Cook 5 minutes more until crumbs have  toasted, remove from heat and allow to cool. Add parsley and stir through.  Divide the crumb mixture among the swordfish pieces and roll up like a  jelly roll and secure it with a toothpick.    In a 2 quart saucepan, stir together tomato sauce, remaining pine nuts,  currants, Sicilian olives, hot chilies and white wine and bring to a boil.  Pour sauce into an oven-proof earthenware dish and arrange involtini over  and in sauce.  Place in oven and bake 25 minutes, until fish is cooked  through. Remove and allow to rest 10 minutes before serving.
