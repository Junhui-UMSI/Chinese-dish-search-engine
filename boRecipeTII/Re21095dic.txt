Two Egg Cake
Eggs, milk, Vanilla, Cake flour; sifted, Granulated sugar, Salt, Baking Powder, Shortening, 
Cream shortening 1 minute at high speed. Add sugar gradually, beating  swiftly. Add eggs, unbeaten, one at a time, and beat well. Add sifted dry  ingredients alternately with the milk to which the vanilla has been added.  Beat well. Pour batter into a loaf pan 8 x 8 x 2 inches, well greased and  lightly floured, or lined with waxed paper. Cut through the batter to break  any large air bubbles. Or if preferred, turn into two greased 8-inch layer  cake pans or into 2 dozen greased cupcake pans. For a loaf cake, bake for  50-60 minutes at 350 degrees F. A layer cake is baked for 25-30 minutes at  375 degrees F. After removal from oven, loosen edges, invert on rack,  removing pan and turn right side up. Cool. Frost with any desired frosting.    Per serving: 261 Calories; 12g Fat (41% calories from fat); 3g Protein; 36g  Carbohydrate; 45mg Cholesterol; 198mg Sodium Food Exchanges: 1  Starch/Bread; 2 Fat; 1 1/2 Other Carbohydrates    NOTES : This recipe comes from a 1950 American Family Cook Book that I  found at an antique shop when I was out looking for old cookbooks to add to  my collection.
