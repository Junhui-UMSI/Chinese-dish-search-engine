Tortilla Casserole From Lhj
Prepared salsa; optional, Salt, Black Pepper, Green pepper; diced, Part-skim ricotta cheese, Flour, Evaporated skim milk; (12, Egg whites; lightly beaten, Vegetable oil; divided, Pickled jalape?o chile;, Corn tortillas; cut in half, Red pepper; diced, Onions, Frozen corn; thawed, Garlic, Eggs, Vegetable cooking spray, 
1. Heat oven to 350F. Lightly coat a 1 1/2- or 2-quart casserole with  vegetable cooking spray. Heat 1 teaspoon oil in a large nonstick skillet  over medium heat. Add peppers and cook 4 to 5 minutes until softened.  Transfer to large bowl. Stir in ricotta cheese, eggs, egg whites and  jalape?o.    2. Add remaining 1 teaspoon oil, onions, corn and garlic to same skillet;  cook 4 to 5 minutes over medium heat, until onions are softened. Stir in  flour; cook 1 minute. Add milk, salt and pepper; cook, stirring, 3 minutes,  until thickened. Stir milk mixture into ricotta mixture until well blended.    3. Spread half the tortilla strips over bottom of prepared casserole. Top  with half the ricotta mixture; repeat layering with remaining strips and  filling. Bake 40 minutes, until lightly browned and firm in center.    Serve with salsa, if desired.    PER SERVING Calories 290 Total Fat 10 g Saturated Fat 4.5 g Cholesterol 95  mg Sodium 631 mg Carbohydrates 32 g Protein 19 g    Notes: This savory, satisfying one-dish meal made with sweet peppers, corn  and pickled jalape?o chile is loaded with flavor. But the biggest  nutritional boost comes from the evaporated skim milk and ricotta, which  supply nearly half of the recommended daily dose of calcium for women.
