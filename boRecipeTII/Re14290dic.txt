White-Chocolate Bread Pudding with Irish Whiskey
Whipping cream, Sugar, Nonfat), Day-old French bread; cut, Bread Pudding:, ", Cold water, Eggs, Cornstarch, Into 1", Milk (do not use low-fat or, Lengthwise, White chocolate; cut into, Irish Whiskey, Sugar, (about 11 cups), Semisweet chocolate chips, Vanilla bean; split, Eggs, Vanilla extract, 
For Sauce: Combine cream, whiskey and sugar in heavy small saucepan.  Scrape in seeds from vanilla bea; add bean. Bring to simmer over high  heat, stirring constantly. Reduce heat to medium-low. Mix 2  tablespoons water and cornstarch in bowl until smooth. Add to cream  mixture; simmer until smooth. Add to cream mixture; simmer until  sauce thickens slightly and leaves path when finger is drawn across  back of spoon, stirring frequently, about 10 minutes. Strain sauce  into small bowl. Cover and refrigerate until well chilled, about 1  hour. For Bread Pudding: Preheat oven to 400 degrees. Whisk milk,  sugar, eggs, yolks and vanilla in large bowl until well blended. Mix  in bread. Let stand 20 minutes. Stir both chocolates into bread  mixture. Transfer to 8-inch square glass baking dish. Place baking  dish in large baking pan. Add enough hot waer to pan to come 1 inch  up sides of baking dish. Bake until pudding is set, about 1 hour.  Remove baking dish from water.
