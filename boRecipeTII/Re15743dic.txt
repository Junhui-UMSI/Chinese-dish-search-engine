Abalone Salad
Fresh shiitake mushrooms, Sm head red leaf lettuce, Chinese pea pods, Limes,or more (juice only), Dark soy sauce, Salt, pepper, Lime or lemon wedges (opt), Small head radicchio**, Black Pepper, Rice vinegar, 1-in. fresh gingerroot ***, Bunch arugula *, Fresh medium abalone, Extra-Virgin Olive Oil, 
Note: If abalone is not available, substitute sea scallops cut into  paper-thin slices. *You may substitute watercress instead of arugula. **May  use red cabbage instead of radicchio. ***Gingerroot should be peeled and  grated. Rinse and dry lettuce, arugula and radicchio. Discard mushroom  stems and slice tops in thin vertical slices. Rinse and dry peas. Remove  abalone from shells, using handle of heavy spoon to break muscle  attachments. Reserve shells. Clean abalone well, discarding undesirable  parts. Clean shells thoroughly, dry and set aside. Using meat slicer, slice  abalone muscle crosswise into tissue-thin pieces. If abalone cant be  sliced tissue-thin, slice thin as possible and pound each slice on flat  surface with mallet or flat end of cleaver until tender and almost  transparent but not shredded. Combine juice of 1 lime, 1 tablespoon grated  ginger and grind or two of fresh pepper in bowl. Add abalone slices and  toss to coat well. Marinate 15 to 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.  Combine remaining lime juice, remaining ginger, vinegar, soy sauce and 3  tablespoons olive oil in small bowl. Tear lettuce, arugula and radicchio  into coarse pieces and place in large bowl. Toss with dressing oil. Arrange  greens in abalone shells or on serving plates. Set aside. Heat remaining 1  tablespoon olive oil in skillet or saute pan, add mushrooms and peas and  season to taste with salt and pepper. Stir-fry briefly, just until peas  turn bright green. Remove from heat and toss to mix well. Pile hot mixture  on top of greens in abalone shells. Top with abalone slices. Garnish with  lime or lemon wedges, if desired.
