Wild Rice
Water, Salt, Basic cooking method., Uncooked wild rice, 
Rinse wild rice in a strainer under running water or in a bowl of water;  drain. In heavy saucepan, bring wild rice, water and salt to a boil; reduce  heat and simmer, covered, until kernels open and are tender but not mushy  (45 to 55 minutes). Drain.    Amount: 3 to 4 cups.    Chicken or beef broth can be substituted for water in cooking wild rice;  omit salt. Variations:    Stir one of the following into cooked wild rice:    1/4 cup melted margarine or butter    1/2 cup chopped onion and 2 cups sliced fresh mushrooms sauteed in 1/4 cup  margarine or butter until tender (2 to 3 minutes)    1/2 cup sliced almonds, sauted in 1/4 cup margarine or butter (2 to 3  minutes)    1/2 cup chopped green onions, sauteed in 1/4 cup margarine or butter until  tender (2 to 3 minutes), 1/4 cup snipped fresh parsley and 2 tablespoons  dry sherry    Recipe by: The Best of Byerlys, vol 2    Posted to MC-Recipe Digest by Kathleen <schuller@ix.netcom.com> on Apr 07,  1998
