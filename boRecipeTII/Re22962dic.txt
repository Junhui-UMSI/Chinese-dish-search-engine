Armagnac Prune Cake
All-purpose flour, Ground cinnamon, Sugar, Ground cloves, Buttermilk, Light corn syrup, Sugar, Unsalted butter, Armagnac or Cognac, Baking soda, Baking soda, Pitted prunes, Water, Armagnac or Cognac, Ground cardamom, Salt, Fresh Lemon Juice, Vanilla ice cream, Eggs, Ground allspice, Vegetable oil, OPTIONAL, Vanilla extract, Ground nutmeg, GLAZE, 
FOR CAKE: Position rack in center of oven and preheat to 350 F. Butter and  flour 12-cup bundt pan.  Combine prunes, water and Armagnac in heavy medium  saucepan. Simmer until prunes are tender, about 15 minutes. Drain prunes,  reserving 1/4 cup cooking liquid for glaze. Coarsely chop prunes.    Beat oil, sugar, eggs and vanilla in large bowl until well blended. In  medium bowl, mix flour, baking soda, spices and salt to blend. Mix dry  ingredients into dry mixture. Add buttermilk, beating until batter is  smooth.  Fold in chopped prunes.  Transfer batter to prepared pan. Bake  cake until tester comes out clean, about 1 hour 5 minutes. Transfer to rack  in pan.    FOR GLAZE:  Combine reserved 1/4 cup prune cooking liquid, sugar, butter,  Armagnac, corn syrup, lemon juice and baking soda in heavy large saucepan.  Bring to boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Boil 2 minutes.    Pierce cake in several places with long toothpick or wooden skewer. Slowly  pour 1-1/4 cups glaze over hot cake. Reserve extra glaze. Let glazed cake  cool in pan 30 minutes. Turn cake out onto platter. Cool completely.    Cut cake into wedges.  Serve with vanilla ice cream, passing additional  glaze separately as sauce.    Bon Appetit - April 1991 Typed for you by Karen Mintzias
