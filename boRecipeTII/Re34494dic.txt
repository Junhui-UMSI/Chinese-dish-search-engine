Stuffed Pork Roast
Celery stalks, chopped, Dried basil, Prepared hot mustard (opt), Dried sage, Raisins, Black Pepper, Boned pork roast (5 lb),, Bulk pork sausage, uncooked, White wine, Honey, STUFFING, Dried thyme, divided, Salt, Bread crumbs, moistened with, ROAST, Green apples, peeled/chopped, Butter, Arrowroot, White wine, Onion, minced, 
From "The Clay-Pot Cookbook" (Macmillan Publishing, $7.95) by George  Macleod Sales and her husband, Grover.    Saute onion and celery in butter until slightly transparent. In large  bowl, combine chopped apple, sausage, raisins, 1 teaspoon salt, dash  of pepper, sage and basil. Add moistened bread crumbs and onion-celery  mixture, and combine well. Pre-soak a clay pot, top and bottom, in  water for 15 minutes. Lay open a roast and sprinkle with 1 teaspoon  thyme. Spread cavity of roast with stuffing; press firmly. Roll roast  and tie with unwaxed dental floss or butchers string. Rub outside of  roast with a mixture of thyme and pepper, then place stuffed roast in  pot. Combine wine, honey and mustard and pour over roast. PLace  covered pot in a cold oven. Set temperature at 480F. Cook for about  2 hours, or until thermometer registers 160 degrees. Remove pot from  oven and pour off liquid into a saucepan. Skim or siphon surface fat  from liquid and heat, Mix arrowroot with 2 tablespoons wine and stir  into sauce.
