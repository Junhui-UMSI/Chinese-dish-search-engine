Stuffed Goose Breast with Caraway and Apples
Double goose breast, removed, Olive oil, plus 4 T, Boiled potatoes, peeled, in, Egg, Fresh rosemary leaves,, Red wine vinegar, Bread crumbs, Italian parsley, finely, Juniper berries, smashed, Caraway seeds, Onions, in 1/4" dice, Green apples, peeled, cored,, Cloves, 
Lay breast out in brownie pan. In a mixing bowl, stir together juniper,  rosemary, olive oil and vinegar and pour over breast. Cover and refrigerate  for 24 hours.    In a 14-inch saute pan, heat remaining oil over medium heat and add onions.  Cook until softened, about 8 to 10 minutes. Add apples, caraway and cooked  potatoes and cook another 10 minutes, or until apples have started to  soften. Remove from heat and allow to cool. Add bread crumbs, parsley,  cloves and egg and season with salt and pepper and set aside.    Preheat oven to 425 degrees. Remove goose from marinade, brush off and pat  dry. Season with salt and pepper and lay flat on cutting board. Lay  stuffing out evenly over goose and roll up like a jelly roll. Tie securely  with butchers twine and place in roasting pan. Roast in oven for 45 to 50  minutes, or until internal temperature reaches 150 degrees. Remove, allow  to rest 10 minutes and carve. Serve with spiced white cabbage.    Yield: 4 servings    Recipe by: Molto Mario MB1D10 Posted to MC-Recipe Digest V1 #593 by Sue  <suechef@sover.net> on May 03, 1997
