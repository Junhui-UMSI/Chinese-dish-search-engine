Vegetable Soup
Wash, Florettes, Whole), Carrot (or two), thinly, Sliced, Russet potatoes cut into 1, Or so herbs: sage, marjoram,, Well), halved or quartered, Onion, cut into chunks, Inch pieces, Tomatoes or 1-1 1/2 cup, Garlic powder, Mushrooms (aged ones work, Segments, Tomato and 1/2 cup canned, Cloves garlic (diced or, 7 cups water, 2 cups cauliflower, cut into, Greenbeans, cut into 1-inch, Basil, thyme, oregano, Tamari/soy sauce, Cut into 1 inch segments, 1/2 -2 inch pieces, Bay Leaves, 2 zucchini, quartered and, Canned tomatoes, Stalks celery, cut into, Spinach, remove stems and, 
In a large pot place onion, carrot, celery, spices, garlic, soy sauce,  garlic powder, bay leaves and water.  Bring to a boil and cook 15 minutes.  Add tomato, mushrooms and green beans and cook for 15 more minutes. Add  potatoes and more water if needed. In 15 more minutes add the cauliflower  and zucchini. Adjust seasonings and add salt to taste (or more soy sauce).  When everything is cooked, remove from heat. Add spinach, making sure to  submurse it in order to wilt the leaves. Serve.    Amounts of vegetables are aproximate, as are cooking times. The soup needs  to cook around 1 hour or 1 hour 15 minutes, so gauge when to add vegetables  by how little you want them to be cooked (or overcooked). This is a hearty  but light soup, so chunk vegetables in large pieces.    From: tara@starburst.umd.edu (Tara McDermott). Fatfree Digest [Volume 10  Issue 1] August 11, 1994.
