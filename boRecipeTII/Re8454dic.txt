Potato, Bacon and Gruyere Soup
Bacon, Gruyere cheese, All purpose flour, Onions, Madeira, Water, Worcestershire Sauce, fresh parsley leaves, Boiling potatoes, Chicken broth, 
In a heavy kettle (at least 5 quarts) cook bacon over moderate heat,  stirring, until crisp and spoon off all but 1 tbsp fat. Add onions and  saute over moderately high heat, stirring, until pale golden. Add broth and  3 cups of water and bring to a boil. Peel potatoes and cut into 3/4" cubes.  Add potatoes to soup and simmer, covered, 10 mins. In a small bowl whisk  together flour and remaining 1/4 cup water until smooth and add to  simmering soup, whisking. Simmer soup, covered, 5 mins. In a blender puree  Gruyere with 3 cups hot soup broth. Stir puree into soup with Madeira,  Worcestershire sauce and salt and pepper to taste and remove soup from  heat. (Soup may be prepared up to this point 3 days ahead, cool uncovered  vefore chilling covered. Reheat over low heat but do not let boil. Just  before serving, stir in parsley. Makes about 10 cups.
