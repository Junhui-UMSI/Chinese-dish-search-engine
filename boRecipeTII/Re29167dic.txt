Baked Salmon Provencale
White wine, olive oil, (2 teaspoons) saffron, Tarragon, Salmon steaks; 3/4-inch, Sage, Greek olives; pitted and, Unsalted butter, Bay leaves crushed, Coarsely chopped plum, Thyme, Garlic, Herbal salt substitute, 
Recipe By: the California Culinary Academy Preparation Time: 0:30    1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Wash and pat dry salmon steaks.    2. In a large, deep, ovenproof skillet or stovetop casserole over  medium-high heat, saute salmon briefly on each side in butter and oil  (about 1 minute). Remove to a platter. Add saffron, garlic, tarragon,  thyme, sage, bay leaves, tomatoes, olives, wine, fish stock, and salt  substitute. Bring to a boil. Lower heat and simmer for 10 minutes,  uncovered.    3. Add salmon steaks. Remove pan from heat and place in oven. Bake until  salmon is lightly pink and done to taste (10 minutes).    4. To serve, place salmon steaks on platter and spoon sauce over them.    Posted to JEWISH-FOOD digest V97 #325 by BNLImp@a0l.com (Iara Lewin) on
