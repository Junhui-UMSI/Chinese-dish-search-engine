Texas Red
Salt, Masa harina, Beef stock, Suet, oregano, dry, Cold water, cumin seeds, Lean beef, Chili powder, Cayenne pepper, Garlic, 
Fat grams    per serving:              Approx. Cook Time: 3hrs Fry suet in  a large heavy kettle until crisp. Then add beef, about 1 pound at a time,  and brown, stiring as it cooks. Remove each pound after browning. When all  meat is browned, return it to kettle and add seasonings and beef stock or  broth. Cover and simmer 1 1/2 - 2 hours. Skim off fat. Combine masa harina  or corn meal with cold water and stir thoroughly into chili. Simmer 30  minutes. Makes about 3 3/4 quarts. "Chili pangs" could strike a man  whenever the skies got grey and the wind turned cold. A plate of Texas Red  was a sure cure. Hot and hearty, it was the kind of chili that warmed a  cowhands belly and brought him back for more. - Famous Chili Recipes from  Marlboro Country.
