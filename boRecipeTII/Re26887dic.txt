Baked Beef Curry with Custard Topping (Bobotie)
Soft bread crumbs, Onions, Salt, Raisins, Pepper, Eggs, Paprika, Ground beef, or lamb, Curry powder, milk, Lemon juice, Slivered almonds; chopped, milk, 
Mix beef, bread crumbs, 1 cup milk, 1 egg, the onion, almonds, raisins,  lemon juice, curry powder (you can use 2-3 teaspoons), salt and pepper.  Spread mixture in ungreased 2-quart casserole.  Cook uncovered in 325  degree oven 45 minutes; drain excess fat.  Mix beaten eggs and 1 cup milk;  pour over beef mixture.  Sprinkle with paprika. Place casserole in oblong  pan, 13 x 9 x 2 inches, on oven rack.  Pour very hot water, (1 inch) into  pan.  Cook uncovered until beef is done and custard is set, about 30  minutes.  Garnish with lemon slices and pimiento if desired. The Dutch settlement  of South Africa    quicked followed, and the flavor of curry made with Indian spices    attracted many South African fans.  Today bobotie is as characteristic    of the food of South Africa as the tortilla is of Mexico.
