To Make Oyle [Sic] of Camomile
Camomile [sic] flowers, Oyle, 
"- take oyle a pint and an halfe [sic], and three ounces of camomile  flowers dryed [sic] one day after they be gathered. Then put the oyle and  the flowers in a glasse [sic] and stop the mouth close and set into the Sun  by the space of forty days."    Cathys note:  I do not know if this is edible.    From _The Good Housewifes Handbook_, 1588.  In Steven Fosters "Chamomile"  article in "The Herb Companion." Dec. 1992/Jan. 1993, Vol. 5, No. 2. Pg.  68.  Posted by Cathy Harned.
