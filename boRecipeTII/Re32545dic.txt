Sweet Green Tomato and Corn Piccalilli
Cloves, White vinegar, Green tomatoes; thin sliced, Red onion, Sugar, each about baseball size, uncooked, Salt, Green bell pepper, Ground cinnamon, Red bell pepper; diced small, diced small, Kernels from 3 ears corn, 
Rub tomato slices with salt and allow to stand overnight, covered and  refrigerated.  In the morning, squeeze the tomato slices between layers of  cheesecloth to remove excess moisture.  Set aside.    In a large saucepan combine the vinegar, sugar, cloves and cinnamon. Bring  to a boil.  Remove from the heat. Add reserved tomatoes; stir well. Add the  remaining ingredients and mix thoroughly.    This piccalilli will keep, covered and refrigerated, several weeks.    Yield: 6 to 8 cups, depending on the size of the tomatoes.    From _Salsas, Sambals, Chutneys & Chowchows_ by Chris Schlesinger and John  Willoughby.  In Alice Colombos 09/21/94 "Cooks Corner" column in "The  (Louisville, KY) Courier-Journal." Pg. C6.
