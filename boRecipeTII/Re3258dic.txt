Pear or Apple Crisp
Sugar, Ground nutmeg, Water, Butter, Whipping cream, Lemon juice, Ground cinnamon, All-purpose flour, Anjou pears, 
Place pears in a greased 8-inch-square baking pan. Sprinkle with cinnamon,  nutmeg, lemon juice, and water; mix lightly.    In a medium-size bowl, mix sugar and flour. Using a pastry blender or 2  knives, cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs; evenly scatter  crumbs over pear mixture. Bake in a 350 degrees oven until fruit is tender  when pierced and crust is lightly browned, about 1 hour. Offer cream to  pour over individual portions.    This recipe appeared in a 1949 issue of Sunset. Pears or apples are lightly  spiced, then baked beneath a crust of sweet, buttery crumbs.    Per serving: 622 cal. (35 percent from fat); 4 g protein; 24 g fat (14 g  sat.); 104 g carbo.; 236 mg sodium; 62 mg chol.
