Pork Tenderloin with Pear Sauce
Dijon mustard, Pepper, Salt, Cornstarch, Nutmeg, Butter, Ground nutmeg, Dried thyme leaves, Shallot; chopped, Cinnamon, Pork tenderloin, Pear; cored, unpeeled,, Marsala wine, 
submitted by: bmarks@pipeline.com (Bev)  Heres one thats great for a dinner party.    In a food processor, combine mustard, shallot, nutmeg, thyme, salt,  cinnamom and pepper; process until creamy. Rub over pork. Place tenderloin  in roasting pan and roast in a 375 degree oven for 25 to 35 minutes.    To make sauce, melt butter in saucepan.  Add pear slices and saute for 1  minute.  Mix wine, cornstarch and nutmeg until smooth; stir into saucepan.  Simmer, stirring constantly for 2 to 3 minutes until thickened. When  tenderloin is cooked, slice and spoon sauce over slices. Makes 4 servings.    DAVE <DAVIDG@CLAM.RUTGERS.EDU>    RECIPEINTERNET LIST SERVER    RECIPE ARCHIVE - 27 MAY 1996    From the RECIPEinternet: Recipes from Around the World recipe list.
