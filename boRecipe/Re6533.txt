  "Instructions": "Halve, peel, and core the pears. Sprinkle freshly cut pears with lemon  juice to prevent browning. Place in microwave-safe dish and cover with  cranapple juice. Cover and cook on high 6 - 8 minutes or until pears are  fork tender. (Check the pears after 6 minutes; the fresher the pears, the  longer theyll take to cook.) Spoon juice over pears, cover, and let stand  30 minutes or refrigerate overnight.    I love the texture and buttery flavor of poached pears. This microwave  poaching method is so simple that sometimes I buy a big bag of pears and  poach them all at once. They can then be refrigerated and covered and eaten  as snacks throughout the next week. Bosc and Bartlett pears are the best  varieties for poaching.    This is a nice holiday recipe because the cranberry juice turns the pears a  pale pink color. These pears are pretty garnished with some fresh mint  sprigs. This recipe is best made at least 30 minutes in advance to give the  pears time to cool.
