  "Instructions": "Preheat the oven to 400 degrees.  In a 9-inch pie pan, combine the crumbs,  pralines and butter together until the mixture is bibded together. Spread  and press the mixture evenly on the bottom and sides of the dish to make  crust.  Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until it is brown. Remove from oven and  cool completely. For the filling: In a non-reactive sauce pan, combine  milk, sugar, vanilla, and syrup over medium heat. Stir slowly to dissolve  the sugar and  heat long enough to scald the milk. Remove from the heat. In  a mixing bowl, combine egg yolks with the cornstarch and mix well. Add 1/4  cup of the milk mixture at a time to the egg mixture, blending in between  each addition, until all is combined. Pour the mixture into a saucepan and  over medium heat, stir constantly for 1-2 minutes. Stir until smooth and  thick.  Remove from heat and put through a fine sieve to remove any lumps.  Chill the filling in an ice bath until cold. Fold in the pralines and  spread evenly into the pie crust. Refrigerate for 2 hours or until it sets.  For the whipping cream: Using an electric mixer, whip the cream, sugar ans  syrup until stiff peaks form. Spread the cream evenly of the top of the  pie.  Slice a piece of the pie and place on plate. Garnish with pralines,  chocolate sauce, powdered sugar and fresh mint sprigs.    Source: Essence of Emeril, #2324, TVFN formatted by Lisa Crawford, 4/29/96    ",
