In small saucepan, heat water and oil until very warm (120 to 130 degrees).  Add warm liquid and egg to flour mixture. Blend at low speed until  moistened; beat 3 minutes at medium speed. Add bean mixture; beat until  well mixed. By hand, stir in green chilies and an additional 3-1/2 to 4  cups flour to form a stiff dough.    On floured or cornmeal-coated surface, knead in 1/2 to 1 cup flour for  about 5 minutes or until dough is smooth and elastic. Place dough in  greased bowl; cover loosely with greased plastic wrap and cloth towel. Let  rise in warm place (80 to 85 degrees) for 45 to 60 minutes or until light  and doubled in size.    Grease two 8 or 9 inch round cake pans or one large cookie sheet. Punch  down dough. Divide dough in half; shape each half into a 6 inch round loaf.  Place in greased pans. Using a sharp knife, 1-1/2 inches from edge, cut  deep ring around each loaf to the bottom. Cut another deep ring 1 inch from  the first cut. Insert cheese cubes deep into cuts; pinch edges to  completely seal. Cover; let rise in warm place 30 to 40 minutes or until  light and almost doubled in size.    Heat oven to 375 degrees. Uncover dough. Bake for 35 to 45 minutes or until  loaves are deep golden brown and sound hollow when lightly tapped.  Immediately remove from pans; cool on wire racks.
