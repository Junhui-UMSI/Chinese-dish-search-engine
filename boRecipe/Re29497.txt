  "Instructions": "Skin  the  leg of lamb and trim away all the outside fat and as much  of  the fat lying between the muscle tissue that you can reach.   Score  the  flesh  deeply  with a criss-cross pattern and stuff the slits  with  the  tarragon.  Rub the meat with the oil and cover with the onion.  Place in  a  suitable  dish for marinating and pour over the white  wine.   Add  a  little salt and pepper and leave to marinate for about 2 hours in a cool  place,  basting occasionally.  Roast the lamb with the marinade, basting  it  frequently,  in a warm oven 325 deg F until done.   About 10 minutes  before the meat is cooked,  pour off the marinade and meat juices into a  saucepan.   Reduce  the gravy to half its original quantity  by  boiling  vigorously.   Carve  the  meat  into thin slices and add  the  resulting  juices  to the marinade.   Arrange the meat on a serving dish  and  keep  warm.   Remove the gravy from the heat,  stir in cream and carefully re-  heat  the  sauce until it forms a medium-thick  consistency.   Pour  the  sauce over the lamb and keep warm.
