  "Instructions": "Put the tepid water in your electric mixer bowl and dissolve the yeast. Use  a paper lunch sack for weighing out the flour. Put the whole-wheat and rye  flour in the bag first, and then make up the weight with the white flour.  Put 4 cups from the bag into the mixer and whip it for 10 minutes. Add the  salted water. If you have a KitchenAid, allow the dough hook to do the rest  of the work. Otherwise, add remaining flour by hand. Knead until the dough  is smooth and elastic. Put the dough on a plastic counter and cover with an  inverted steel bowl. Allow it to rise once, punch it down, and allow it to  rise a second time. Punch down and form into 2 or 3 loaaves. I never use  bread pans for this, as they will ruin the crust. Place the loaves on  baking sheets that have been dusted with cornmeal and allow the loaves to  rise until double in bulk. Bake in a 450 degree oven about 25 minutes, or  until the crust is golden and the loaf light to the touch. It should make a  hollow sound when you thump your finger on the bottom of the loaf.    Serving Ideas : Makes 2 or 3 loaves.    NOTES : See Italian Breadsticks and Crustulum (Bruschetta) for other ways  to use this bread recipe.
