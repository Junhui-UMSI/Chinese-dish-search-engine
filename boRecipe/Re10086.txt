  "Instructions": "Make stuffing by combining bread, currants, apples, thyme, salt, pepper and  melted butter. Stuff, truss and tie goose. Prick bird all over with fork.  Heat oil in roasting pan on top of stove, brown goose lightly on all sides,  then drain off pan drippings. Set goose breast side up, add a little water,  cover and roast at 375 degrees for one hour. Combine chopped onion, carrot,  celery, the garlic, bay leaf, cloves, thyme and marjoram. Discard fat from  roasting pan, add vegetable mixture and continue roasting uncovered 20 - 25  minutes per pound (three to four hours in all) draining off fat at  intervals and adding more water as required. Transfer cooked goose to  platter and keep warm. Skim off remaining fat in pan and heat dripping and  vegetables on top of stove until mixture is reduced. Then stir in white  wine, tomato paste and chicken bouillon. Simmer for 10 - 15 minutes, then  strain gravy. A little cornstarch mixed with water may be blended in to  thicken gravy, if desired. Serve goose with gravy, applesauce, mashed  potatoes and braised cabbage. Six to eight servings. From The Gazette  90/12/19.    ",
