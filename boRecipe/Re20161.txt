  "Instructions": "In a standing mixer with paddle, cream butter and sugar until light and  fluffy. Split and scrape vanilla bean into the mixture. *Do not use pod -  only the seeds. Mix well and add whites and flour. Mix well and chill  covered with plastic wrap, preferably overnight. *Use pod for compote.    Combine all ingredients in a heavy bottomed saucepan. Bring to a boil.  Reduce heat and simmer 5 to 8 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool in  pan. Remove all fruit and reduce sauce until syrup-like. Combine with  fruit. Remove pod and set aside until ready to assemble.    Baking: Spread about 1 teaspoon cold tuille batter thinly over stencils  (professional or homemade) on greased back of cookie sheet. Place tuiles in  350 degree F oven. Tuiles will spread and cook rapidly. Therefore, test a  few, one at a time, for appropriate thinness of batter and length of  cooking time. If tuiles have spread too much, reduce amount of batter and  make sure batter is spread evenly and thinly. Tuiles should be removed from  oven when slightly golden. Let set for a few seconds on sheet before  removing to rack for cooling.    Assemble Napoleon by layering tuiles and fruit compote. Top with ice cream,  frozen yogurt, or whipped cream.
