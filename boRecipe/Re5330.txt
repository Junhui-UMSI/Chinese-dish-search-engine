  "Instructions": "1.  Place 1 1/2 Tbs each of the almonds and cashews and all of the sesame  seeds, coriander seeds, and coconut in a blender or food processor, cover  and pulse to a coarse powder.  Add 1/2 cup of coconut milk and the ginger  and chilies, cover and process until smooth.  Pour through a strainer,  pressing out all of the liquid.  (You can substitute water for coconut milk  if you like.  I much prefer the coconut milk, though).    2.  Heat the ghee or butter in a 3 quart/liter saucepan over moderate heat.  Add the remaining 1 1/2 Tbs. each cashews and almonds and stir-fry until  golden brown, then remove them with a slotted spoon and set aside.  Drop in  the black mustard seeds and fry until they turn gray and pop.  Pour in the  seed-nut milk and cook, stirring to prevent sticking, for 2-3 minutes. Pour  in the remaining 1 1/4 cups water or coconut milk and the saffron threads  and salt.  Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes.    3.
