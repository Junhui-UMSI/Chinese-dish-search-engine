  "Instructions": "These are often called thousand-year eggs, even though the preserving  process lasts only 100 days. They may be purchased individually in Oriental  markets.        Combine tea, salt, ashes and lime. Using about 1/2 cup per egg, thickly  coat each egg completely with this clay-like mix- ture. Line a large crock  with garden soil and carefully lay coated eggs on top. Cover with more soil  and place crock in a cool dark place. Allow to cure for 100 days. To remove  coating, scrape eggs and rinse under running water to clean thoroughly.   Crack lightly and remove shells.
