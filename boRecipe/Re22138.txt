  "Instructions": "My note: Remove bones from turkey or chicken before serving and  refrigerate or freeze until you have time to prepare the soup. I like  to do it the next day, if possible, and make a batch of crackers  while the soup is cooking.    STOCK: In stockpot or large saucepan, combine bones, water, bay leaf,  celery and onion. (Skin and drippings may be included, if desired.)  Simmer, covered about 4 hours. Strain, reserving stock. Let bones  cool, pick out any meat and add to the stock.    SOUP: In stockpot or saucepan, bring stock to boil; add noodles and  simmer for 5 minutes. Add celery, carrot, green onions, zucchini,  basil and thyme. Simmer for 10 minutes.  Stir in hot pepper sauce,  season with salt and pepper to taste.  Makes about 4 1/2 cups.    3/4 cup serving - 91 calories, 1 1/2 protein, 1/2 fruit & vegetable  choice 5 grams carbohydrate, 11 grams protein, 3 grams fat 137 mg  sodium, 32 mg cholesterol    Source:  The Lighthearted Cookbook by Anne Lindsay, Cdn. Heart  Foundation 1988.  Shared but not tested by Elizabeth Rodier, Nov 93    VARIATIONS: Substitute rice for noodles. Use 6 cups chicken stock if  you start without bones For a main course, add 19 oz can chickpeas or  kidney beans, drained.
