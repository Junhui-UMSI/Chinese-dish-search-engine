  "Instructions": "In a nonstick large saucepan, brown the sausage. Remove and let cool.  Coarsely chop.    While the sausage cool, brown the onion in olive oil, stirring frequently  until soft and sweet. Add garlic and pepper. Add the potatoes, fennel  stalks, and bacon bits. Stir to warm. Then add the water or broth. Season  with bouillon base and a sprig of tarragon. Bring to a boil and simmer for  15 to 20 minutes. Remove the tarragon sprig. Mash some of the potatoes to  thicken the soup. Add the sausage, finely shredded cabbage or kale and  spinach. Add water, if needed. Taste and add chopped oregano to taste  (about 1 teaspoon fresh, 1/4 teaspoon dried.) Heat gently (do not boil) for  about 8 to 10 minutes. Serve. PER SERVING: 160.8 cals, 5.5 g fat (29.9%  cff).    A country soup with the intriguing tastes of fennel and tarragon; similar  to Olive Gardens Toscana. Dec 97 /path    TIP: Some may wish to keep the fennel weed (leaves) whole so that they may  be removed before serving. Try Jimmy Deans Turkey Sausage or the Turkey  Stores Italian, casings removed.
