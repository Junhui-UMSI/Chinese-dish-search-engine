Faye Kuhn, of Earlville, Illinois, recalled growing up in the tough times  of the 40s: \"Despite the lack of finances, no holiday was ever ignored in  our household. Christmas, however, held the highest of honors. . . We all  boarded the train and off to the city we would go. We would arrive home  loaded with Evening in Paris cologne, mittens, socks, handkerchiefs and  coloring books bought at Kresges and Woolworths.
