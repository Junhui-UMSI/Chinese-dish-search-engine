  "Instructions": "Submitted by: Cookie Recipe.com \r\n\r\nStaff    This Near Eastern pastry is made of many layers of paper-thin dough with a  filling usually of honey and ground nuts. If you like honey, youll  probably like Baklava.   \r\n\r\n 1 Preheat oven to 450 degrees F.    \r\n\r\n2 Melt the butter over low heat. Pour 2 tablespoons of the butter into the  bottom of a 13 x 9 inch baking pan. Layer 3 sheets of the phyllo dough in  the pan. Trim dough to fit.   \r\n\r\n 3 Sprinkle 2 tablespoons of pecans over the phyllo dough.    \r\n\r\n4 Layer 3 more sheets of dough and sprinkle with 2 tablespoons of pecans.  Continue dough - pecan layers until pan is 3/4 full.    \r\n\r\n5 With a sharp knife, score phyllo dough to form diamonds. Press a clove at  each end of the diamonds. Pour remaining butter over the dough.    \r\n\r\n6 Bake 45 to 50 minutes, until dough is golden brown.   \r\n\r\n7 While dough is baking, combine the sugar, water and cinammon stick in  medium saucepan and bring to boil, stirring constantly. Simmer for 10  minutes.    \r\n\r\n8 Add the honey and simmer for 2 minutes longer. Remove from heat and  discard cinnamon stick. \r\n\r\n9 Pour honey mixture over hot baklava. Let cool on  wire racks. Cut into diamonds.    \r\n\r\nMakes 2 dozen  \r\n\r\nPosted to recipelu-digest Volume 01 Number 559 by \"Valerie Whittle\"  ~~ltcatspaw~~at;inetnow.net> on Jan 19, 1998",
