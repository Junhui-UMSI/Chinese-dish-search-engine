  "Instructions": "Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F. Roll out half the dough 1/8-inch thick  on a lightly floured surface, fit it into a 9-inch (1-quart) glass pie  plate, and trim the edge, leaving a 3/4-inch overhang. Chill the shell and  the remaining dough while making the filling. In a large bowl toss together  the apples, each peeled, cored, and cut into eighths, 3/4 cup of the sugar,  flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and lemon juice until the mixture is  combined well. Transfer the filling to the shell, and dot it with the  butter.    Roll out the remaining dough into a 13- to14-inch round on a lightly  floured surface, drape it over the filling, and trim it, leaving a 1-inch  overhang. Fold the overhang under the bottom crust, pressing the edge to  seal it, and crimp the edge decoratively. Brush the crust lightly with the  milk, cut slits in it with a sharp knife, forming steam vents, and sprinkle  the pie evenly with the remaining 1 tablespoon sugar. Bake the pie on a  large baking sheet in the middle of the oven for 20 minutes, reduce the  temperature to 350 degrees F, and bake the pie for 20 to 25 minutes more,  or until the crust is golden and the apples are tender.
